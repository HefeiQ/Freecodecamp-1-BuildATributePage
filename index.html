Jiang Yang

"the most virtuous wife, the most talented woman"

Jiang Yang(杨绛) in 1941

Here's a time line of Yang's life:

  • 1911 - Born in Beijing, China
  • 1932 - Graduated from Soochow University and enrolled in the graduate school of Tsinghua University where she met her husband Zhongshu Qian.
  • 1935~1938 - They went abroad to England for further study at Oxford University and the University of London.At that time, she gave birth to their daughter Qian Yuan (钱媛). They later studied at Pantheon-Sorbonne University in Paris, France.
  • 1938 - Came back to China.
  • 1944 - Wrote plays in the "anti-romantic" style: As You Wish
  • 1945 - Wrote plays in the "anti-romantic" style: Taking True for False
  • 1947 - Wrote plays in the "anti-romantic" style: Quilts in the Wind
  • 1953 - Started to teach at the Tsinghua University and made a scholarly study of western literature at Peking University and the Academy of Science.
  • 1951 - Translated Lazarillo de Tormes into Chinese.
  • 1956 - Translated Gil Blas into Chinese.
  • 1978 - Translated Don Quixote into Chinese which is, as of 2016, still considered the definitive version.
  • 1981 - Published Six Chapters from My Life 'Downunder'.
  • 1981 - Published Soon to Have Tea.
  • 1986 - Awarded the Civil Order of Alfonso X, the Wise for this by King Juan Carlos.
  • 1988 - Published her only novelBaptism, which was always connected with Fortress Besieged, a masterpiece of her husband.
  • 2003 - Published her memoirWe Three, recalled memories of her husband and her daughter Qian Yuan, who died of cancer one year before her father's death in 1998.
  • 2007 - Published Reaching the Brink of Life, a philosophic work whose title in Chinese clearly alludes to her late husband's collection of essays Marginalia to Life.
  • 2016 - Dies at the age of 105.

"The most virtuous wife, the most talented woman.""Wife, lover, and friend."

--Zhongshu Qian, her husband, a well-known Chinese literary scholar and writer
